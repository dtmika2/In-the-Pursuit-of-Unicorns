How Attributes Work

When something in the world needs to be decided, the player and DM agree on which attribute resolves it. The DM sets a difficulty, and you must meet or beat it by rolling a d20 and adding your attribute modifier. The modifier is derived from the attribute's full value - for example, Constitution (16) gives a modifier of +2.

Example

The bridge over the gorge has rotted through. The gap is eight feet wide. You decide to run and leap. The DM calls for a Constitution check - difficulty 14. You roll a d20 and get 11. You add your Constitution modifier of +2.

d20 (11) + Constitution (+2) = 13  X  You don't clear the gap.
How Talents Work

When a roll clearly falls within a talent's scope, add +1 on top of your attribute modifier. The DM decides whether it applies - when in doubt: is the activity specific enough that talent should be used?

Same jump - if the use of talent is justified
Athletics insted of Constitution: d20 (11) + Athletics (+2) + Talent Used (+1) = 14  ✓  You clear the gap.

The main difference between Talent and Attribute is that you can gain a small bonus by using specific Talent in justified situations and you can also rise few specific Talents above your base attributes when creating character.

While creating your character, choose 1 talent from your class attribute and 2 from your race attributes as starting talents. If a talent is your starting talent (chosen through class or race), its modifier is +1 higher than the parent attribute. A starting talent under an attribute with modifier −1 becomes 0 - still worth having. All other talents simply match the modifier of their parent attribute.
