How Attributes Work

When something in the world needs to be decided, you the player and DM propose which attribute the character uses to resolve it. DM sets a difficulty of that task and player must overcome it by rolling a d20. To your roll, you add your attribute modifier. Attribute modifier is driven from the Attributes whole value. Example: Cunningness (16) -> modifier (+2).

 
How Talents Work

When a roll clearly falls within a talent's scope, add +1 to the total. This stacks with your attribute modifier.

Example: d20 + modifier (+x) + talent (+y) = d20 + x + y
Example: 16 + Cunningness (+2) + Deceit (+1) = d20 + 2 + 1 = 19

The DM decides whether a talent applies. When in doubt: if you would describe what you're doing using the talent's name, it applies.

While creating your character, you can choose 1 talent from your class Attribute and 2 from your race Attributes. These gain +1 modifier above your attribute modifier. The rest match the modifier of the attribute.
